<p><p>Track Geometry Measurement Systems are categorized into two main types: no contact and contact systems. No contact systems utilize technologies like laser and imaging to assess track geometry without physical touch, enhancing safety and efficiency. In contrast, contact systems involve sensors or instruments that physically interact with the track, providing precise measurements through direct engagement. Both types serve critical roles in ensuring the integrity and performance of rail infrastructure, aiding in maintenance and operational planning.</p></p>
