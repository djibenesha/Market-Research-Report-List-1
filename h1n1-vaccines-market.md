<p><p>The H1N1 vaccines market consists of three primary types: intramuscular, intranasal, and intradermal vaccines. Intramuscular vaccines, delivered through a needle into a muscle, are the most common, offering robust immunity. Intranasal vaccines are administered via the nasal spray route, providing a needle-free option and eliciting mucosal immunity. Intradermal vaccines are injected into the skin's dermal layer, utilizing a smaller dosage with potentially fewer side effects, enhancing immune response while minimizing the volume of vaccine required.</p></p>
