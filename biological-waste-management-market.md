<p><p>Biological waste management encompasses various methods for disposing of hazardous biological materials. Autoclaving employs steam sterilization at high pressure to eliminate microorganisms, making waste safe for disposal. Other methods include chemical treatment, shredding, and encapsulation, which cater to specific waste types and regulatory requirements. Each method is chosen based on the waste's nature, volume, and the desired level of decontamination, ensuring environmental and public safety.</p></p>
