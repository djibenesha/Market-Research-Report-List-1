<p><p>The non-contact liquid level sensor market includes three main types: ultrasonic, capacitive, and hydrostatic sensors. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to measure liquid levels, providing accurate readings without physical contact. Capacitive sensors detect changes in capacitance caused by the presence of liquid, making them suitable for various liquids, including corrosive types. Hydrostatic sensors measure liquid pressure at a specific depth to determine level, offering reliable data for various applications, including water and chemical tanks.</p></p>
