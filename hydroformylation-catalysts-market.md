<p><p>The hydroformylation catalysts market includes cobalt-based and rhodium-based types, both used to convert alkenes into aldehydes. Cobalt-based catalysts are cost-effective and robust, suitable for high-pressure processes, but require harsh conditions and produce byproducts. Rhodium-based catalysts are more efficient, operate under milder conditions, and offer higher selectivity, but are significantly more expensive. Both types are vital in producing chemicals like plasticizers and detergents, with rhodium dominating high-value applications and cobalt preferred for cost-sensitive industries. The choice depends on process requirements and economic considerations.</p></p>
