<p><p>Inert resins are broadly categorized into alkaline and acidic types based on their pH characteristics and applications. Alkaline resins are used for processes requiring basic conditions, often in ion exchange and separation techniques, facilitating the extraction of specific compounds. Acidic resins, on the other hand, are employed in environments that require an acidic pH, commonly used in catalysis and water treatment to remove cations. Both types play crucial roles in various industrial and environmental applications.</p></p>
